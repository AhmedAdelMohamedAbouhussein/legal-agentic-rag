SUPPLY AGREEMENT
Agreement No. SA-2018-0041

This Supply Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 10, 2018, by and between:

ALPHA CORP, a Delaware corporation ("Buyer")
and
BETASUPPLY INC., a California corporation ("Supplier")

1. SCOPE OF SUPPLY

Supplier agrees to supply electronic components as specified in Schedule A (the "Products") to Buyer for a period of thirty-six (36) months from the Effective Date.

2. PURCHASE ORDERS AND DELIVERY

2.1 Buyer shall issue Purchase Orders with a minimum 30-day lead time.
2.2 Supplier shall confirm receipt within 48 hours and deliver by the specified date.
2.3 Delivery terms: DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) to Buyer's facility in Austin, Texas.

3. PRICING AND PAYMENT

3.1 Unit prices are fixed for Year 1. Years 2 and 3 subject to CPI adjustment not exceeding 3%.
3.2 Payment terms: Net 45 days from invoice date.
3.3 Late payment shall accrue interest at 1.5% per month.

4. QUALITY AND INSPECTION

4.1 All Products must conform to Buyer's technical specifications set forth in Exhibit B.
4.2 Buyer has the right to inspect Products within 10 business days of delivery.
4.3 Rejected Products must be replaced within 15 business days at Supplier's cost.

5. WARRANTIES

5.1 Supplier warrants that all Products: (a) conform to specifications; (b) are free from defects in material and workmanship; (c) are new and not refurbished; (d) do not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights.
5.2 Warranty period: eighteen (18) months from delivery.

6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1 All tooling, dies, and specifications provided by Buyer remain Buyer's property.
6.2 Supplier retains ownership of its pre-existing manufacturing know-how.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 Each party agrees to keep confidential the other party's technical specifications, pricing, and business information.
7.2 This obligation survives termination for five (5) years.

8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1 Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.
8.2 Supplier's total aggregate liability shall not exceed the total fees paid in the 12 months prior to the claim.

9. INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 Supplier shall indemnify Buyer against third-party claims arising from product defects, IP infringement, or Supplier's negligence.

10. TERMINATION

10.1 Either party may terminate for material breach upon 30 days' written notice if such breach remains uncured.
10.2 Buyer may terminate for convenience upon 90 days' written notice.
10.3 Upon termination, Supplier must fill all outstanding Purchase Orders already confirmed.

11. COMPLIANCE

11.1 Supplier shall comply with all applicable laws, including export controls, anti-bribery (FCPA, UK Bribery Act), and modern slavery regulations.
11.2 Supplier shall maintain and provide upon request certifications: ISO 9001, RoHS, REACH.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 The parties shall attempt to resolve disputes through senior management negotiation within 30 days.
12.2 Unresolved disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration under JAMS rules in New York, NY.
12.3 Arbitration award shall be final and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction.
12.4 Governing Law: State of New York, excluding conflict of law provisions.

13. INSURANCE

13.1 Supplier shall maintain: Commercial General Liability ($2M per occurrence), Product Liability ($5M), Workers' Compensation (statutory limits).

14. FORCE MAJEURE

14.1 Neither party shall be liable for failure or delay in performance caused by circumstances beyond their reasonable control, including but not limited to: acts of God, natural disasters, war, terrorism, governmental action, fire, flood, earthquake, or labor strikes.
14.2 The affected party must provide written notice within 5 business days of the force majeure event.
14.3 If the force majeure event continues for more than ninety (90) days, either party may terminate this Agreement without penalty upon 15 days' written notice.
14.4 The affected party must use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact of the force majeure event.

15. GENERAL PROVISIONS

15.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding its subject matter.
15.2 Amendments must be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both parties.
15.3 Waivers must be in writing; failure to enforce any right is not a waiver.
15.4 Severability: If any provision is unenforceable, the remainder continues in full force.
15.5 Notices: To Alpha Corp, Attn: Chief Procurement Officer. To BetaSupply, Attn: VP Sales.
