EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2019, between:

NEXTECH INC., a Delaware corporation ("Company")
and
SARAH CHEN ("Employee")

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1 Position: Senior Software Engineer — SaaS Platform Team
1.2 Reports to: VP of Engineering
1.3 Start Date: March 1, 2019
1.4 Location: San Francisco, CA (remote work permitted up to 3 days/week)

2. COMPENSATION

2.1 Base Salary: $185,000 per year, payable bi-weekly.
2.2 Annual Bonus: Target 15% of base salary, subject to performance and Company discretion.
2.3 Equity: 0.15% of Company fully diluted shares, 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff.
2.4 Benefits: Health, dental, vision, 401(k) with 4% Company match, 20 days PTO.

3. AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT

Employment is at-will. Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, with or without notice, subject to Section 7 below.

4. CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 Employee shall not disclose any Confidential Information of the Company during or after employment.
4.2 "Confidential Information" includes: source code, product roadmaps, customer lists, pricing, financial data, technical know-how, trade secrets, and any information marked confidential.
4.3 Upon termination, Employee shall return all Company materials and delete all copies.
4.4 This obligation survives termination indefinitely.

5. NON-SOLICITATION

5.1 During employment and for twelve (12) months after termination, Employee shall not:
    (a) Solicit, recruit, or hire any Company employee or contractor; or
    (b) Solicit any Company customer or prospective customer with whom Employee had material contact.
5.2 This Section 5 is intended to be enforceable to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law.

6. NON-COMPETITION

6.1 During employment and for twenty-four (24) months after termination, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, or consult for any business that competes with the Company in the SaaS platform market anywhere in the United States.
6.2 Governing Law for Non-Compete: The parties agree that the law of the State of Texas shall govern the enforceability of this Section 6.
6.3 NOTICE: California Labor Code Section 16600 may affect the enforceability of this provision for California-based employees. See Section 14 (Governing Law).

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

7.1 Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to any and all Inventions, whether or not patentable, that Employee may solely or jointly conceive, develop, reduce to practice, or cause to be conceived, developed or reduced to practice during the period of Employee's employment with Company.
7.2 "Inventions" means any invention, innovation, improvement, development, discovery, computer program, device, trade secret, method, know-how, process, technique or work of authorship.
7.3 CALIFORNIA NOTICE: Pursuant to California Labor Code § 2872, the above assignment does not apply to any invention that qualifies for protection under California Labor Code § 2870. A copy of § 2870 is attached as Exhibit A.
7.4 Employee shall promptly disclose all Inventions to the Company in writing.
7.5 Employee shall execute all documents reasonably necessary to evidence and perfect the Company's ownership.

8. MORAL RIGHTS WAIVER

Employee waives all moral rights and rights of attribution with respect to all Inventions assigned hereunder.

9. PRIOR INVENTIONS

Employee represents that Exhibit B lists all prior inventions Employee wishes to exclude from this Agreement. If Exhibit B is blank, Employee represents there are no such prior inventions.

10. NON-DISPARAGEMENT

During and after employment, Employee shall not make disparaging statements about the Company, its products, services, officers, directors, or employees.

11. RETURN OF PROPERTY

Upon termination, Employee shall immediately return all Company property including laptops, access badges, documents, and any copies of Confidential Information.

12. SEVERANCE

12.1 If Employee is terminated without cause, Employee shall receive: (a) 3 months' base salary; (b) COBRA premium coverage for 3 months; and (c) accelerated vesting of 6 months of unvested equity.
12.2 Severance is contingent upon Employee signing a general release of claims.

13. ARBITRATION

All disputes arising from this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration before JAMS in San Francisco under JAMS Employment Arbitration Rules. CLASS AND COLLECTIVE ACTION WAIVER: Employee waives the right to participate in any class or collective action.

14. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to conflict of laws provisions. The parties expressly agree to Texas jurisdiction for all disputes. Notwithstanding the foregoing, if Employee is a California resident, California Labor Code provisions that cannot be waived by private agreement shall apply.

15. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to Employee's employment and supersedes all prior agreements.
